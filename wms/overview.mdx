The Bold Warehouse Management System (WMS) is the operational backbone of your factory floor and warehouse. Where an ERP handles your financials and commercial records, Bold WMS handles what physically happens in your warehouse: where every item is, how it arrived, what it has been used for, and where it is going next. Whether you run a single warehouse or multiple storage zones, the WMS keeps every movement visible, accurate, and auditable — in real time.

## What Bold WMS Does

Bold WMS gives you end-to-end control over your warehouse operations without the complexity of enterprise-grade systems. Its core job is to eliminate the manual admin — spreadsheets, paper forms, line-by-line data entry — that quietly drains your team's time and introduces costly errors.

## WMS vs. ERP: Complementary, Not Competing

It is common to ask whether a WMS overlaps with an ERP. They are designed for different jobs and work best when connected.

| | ERP | WMS |
|---|---|---|
| **Focus** | Administrative, financial, commercial | Physical warehouse operations |
| **Key tasks** | Planning, invoicing, procurement | Receipts, locations, picking, traceability |
| **Where it runs** | Office | Warehouse and shop floor |

The ERP plans and invoices; the WMS executes in the warehouse. Bold connects both through a real-time bidirectional API — purchase orders flow from your ERP into Bold WMS, and every stock movement (receipt, dispatch, inventory adjustment) flows back automatically. No double entry, no discrepancies.

<Note>
  Bold has a public, documented API compatible with the most widely used ERPs, including SAP, Sage, Navision, and A3.
</Note>

## WMS and MES: Closing the Loop

Bold also integrates WMS with its Manufacturing Execution System (MES) and Material Requirements Planning (MRP) in a single cloud platform. This means:

- **Production orders automatically consume raw materials** from the WMS the moment they are started on the shop floor.
- **Finished goods are immediately created in stock** when a production run completes — no manual entry required.
- **MRP purchase suggestions** are based on real-time stock levels from the WMS, not a manually updated spreadsheet.

The result is a factory where the warehouse and the shop floor always speak the same language, and data silos disappear.

<Tip>
  If you are already using Bold MES, your WMS stock is updated in real time with every production event. There is nothing extra to configure.
</Tip>
